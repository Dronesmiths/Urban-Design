Essential Features of Our Accessible Walk-In Showers

Every accessible bathroom we design includes these critical safety and accessibility features

Barrier-Free Entry

Zero-threshold or low-threshold entry allows wheelchair access and eliminates tripping hazards. No step to navigate means safer, easier access for everyone.

Strategically Placed Grab Bars

ADA-compliant grab bars installed at optimal heights and locations for maximum support and stability. Tested to support 250+ lbs for complete safety.

Built-In Seating

Fold-down or permanent shower seats allow comfortable, safe bathing while seated. Perfect for those with limited mobility or balance concerns.

Non-Slip Flooring

Textured, slip-resistant tile flooring provides secure footing even when wet. We use premium materials rated for maximum slip resistance.

Handheld Shower Head

Adjustable handheld shower heads with easy-grip handles allow bathing while seated and provide greater control and independence.

Spacious Design

Minimum 36" x 36" shower space (or larger) provides room for wheelchair turning radius and caregiver assistance when needed.

Temperature Control

Anti-scald valves and easy-to-use temperature controls prevent burns and make operation simple for those with limited dexterity.

Enhanced Lighting

Bright, well-placed LED lighting ensures clear visibility and reduces fall risk. We design lighting that's easy to reach and operate.

Accessible Bathroom Walk-In Shower FAQs

An ADA-compliant walk-in shower meets the Americans with Disabilities Act standards for accessibility. This includes features like barrier-free or low-threshold entry, grab bars at specific heights and locations, non-slip flooring, adequate space for wheelchair access (minimum 36" x 36"), built-in or fold-down seating, and accessible controls. Our team is certified in ADA design standards and ensures every installation meets or exceeds these requirements.

Most accessible walk-in shower installations take 1-2 weeks from start to finish. This includes demolition of the existing tub or shower, plumbing modifications, installation of the new walk-in shower, grab bars, seating, and finishing work.
